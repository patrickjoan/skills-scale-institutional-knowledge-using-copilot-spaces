# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing

### Role Summary
QA Engineers validate that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They design test strategies, execute tests, and identify issues before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans for features and releases
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Report, track, and verify bug fixes
- Collaborate with Developers on testability and automation
- Perform regression testing and end-to-end validation

### Goals
- Ensure product quality and reliability
- Reduce defects reaching production
- Improve test coverage and automation efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report test progress and blockers
- Bug reports and test summaries
- Feedback on acceptance criteria and DoD

### Typical Interactions
- Works with **Developers** on test automation, bug reproduction, and fixes
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** to clarify acceptance criteria
- Reports to **Project Managers** on testing status and release readiness
- Coordinates with **DevOps Engineers** on test environments and CI/CD integration

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with interest in project outcomes. They may include executives, customers, partners, or other teams who provide inputs, requirements, or approvals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and context
- Review and approve project deliverables
- Give feedback on prototypes and releases
- Participate in milestone reviews and demos

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes align with business objectives
- Provide timely feedback and decisions
- Support successful adoption and rollout

### Typical Communication
- Monthly status updates and milestone reviews
- Demo sessions and feedback rounds
- Decision gates and approval checkpoints

### Typical Interactions
- Provides requirements and feedback to **Product Managers** and **Project Managers**
- Reviews demos and deliverables from **Developers** and **UX/UI Designers**
- Receives status updates from **Project Managers**

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies and help teams continuously improve their processes. They remove impediments, coach the team on agile practices, and foster a culture of collaboration and self-organization.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and reviews
- Remove process barriers and organizational impediments
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Protect the team from external distractions
- Track and improve team velocity and process metrics

### Goals
- Enable smooth sprint execution and team self-organization
- Continuously improve team processes and collaboration
- Maximize value delivery through agile practices

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members to address concerns
- Process improvement discussions with management

### Typical Interactions
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** on timelines and resource planning
- Works with **Product Managers** to refine backlog and priorities
- Facilitates communication between **Developers**, **QA**, and other team members
- Escalates impediments to **Project Managers** and leadership

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create user-centered designs that balance usability, aesthetics, and business goals. They research user needs, design interfaces and interactions, and validate designs through testing and feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and analyze user behavior
- Design wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with teams to ensure design feasibility
- Conduct usability testing and iterate on feedback

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Ensure design consistency across products
- Validate designs meet user needs and business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and insights
- Prototype demos and usability test results

### Typical Interactions
- Partners with **Product Managers** to understand user needs and priorities
- Collaborates with **Developers** on design implementation and feasibility
- Works with **Business Analysts** to understand business requirements
- Presents designs to **Stakeholders** for feedback and approval
- Coordinates with **Technical Writers** on UI content and help documentation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They analyze requirements, document processes, and ensure solutions deliver the intended business value.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into technical specifications
- Validate that solutions meet business requirements
- Facilitate communication between business and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure solutions align with business objectives
- Improve clarity and completeness of requirements
- Support data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and business cases
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Process flow diagrams and data analysis

### Typical Interactions
- Works closely with **Product Managers** to refine requirements and priorities
- Translates business needs for **Developers** and technical teams
- Gathers requirements from **Stakeholders** and subject matter experts
- Collaborates with **UX/UI Designers** on user needs and workflows
- Supports **Project Managers** with scope definition and planning

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate documentation that helps users, developers, and support teams understand and use products effectively. They maintain documentation throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Write and maintain user guides, API documentation, and release notes
- Create onboarding materials and tutorials
- Document features, workflows, and troubleshooting guides
- Ensure documentation accuracy and consistency
- Collaborate with teams to understand technical details

### Goals
- Make products easy to understand and use
- Reduce support burden through clear documentation
- Maintain up-to-date, comprehensive documentation

### Typical Communication
- Documentation plans and content reviews
- Release notes and changelog updates
- Feedback sessions with users and support teams

### Typical Interactions
- Works with **Developers** to understand technical implementation and APIs
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** on feature documentation priorities
- Partners with **UX/UI Designers** on UI content and terminology
- Coordinates with **Support & Operations** to address common user questions
- Aligns with **Project Managers** on documentation milestones and releases

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, tools, and automation that enable reliable, efficient software delivery. They focus on CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure management, monitoring, and operational excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure and deployment automation
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure system reliability, security, and performance
- Support incident response and post-mortem analysis

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and safe deployments
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Automate repetitive tasks and improve operational efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Deployment notifications and status updates
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Infrastructure and performance metrics

### Typical Interactions
- Collaborates with **Developers** on build processes and deployment needs
- Works with **QA** to set up test environments and automation
- Partners with **Project Managers** on release schedules and deployment planning
- Coordinates with **Support & Operations** on production issues and monitoring
- Supports **Security** teams with security scanning and compliance

---

## Support & Operations

### Role Summary
Support & Operations teams handle post-release customer issues, monitor production systems, and provide operational feedback to improve product quality and reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to customer support tickets and inquiries
- Monitor production systems and alerts
- Triage and escalate production incidents
- Document common issues and workarounds
- Provide feedback on product usability and reliability

### Goals
- Deliver excellent customer support experience
- Minimize customer-impacting incidents
- Improve product quality through operational feedback

### Typical Communication
- Support ticket updates and resolution notes
- Incident escalations and status updates
- Customer feedback and pain point reports

### Typical Interactions
- Escalates production issues to **DevOps Engineers** and **Developers**
- Provides customer feedback to **Product Managers** for prioritization
- Works with **Technical Writers** to improve documentation and troubleshooting guides
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** on release impact and customer communication
- Coordinates with **QA** on bug reproduction and verification

## RACI Matrix for Key Project Activities

Understanding who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for key activities:

| Activity | PM | PdM | Dev | QA | Scrum Master | UX/UI | BA | Tech Writer | DevOps | Support | Stakeholders |
|----------|----|----|-----|----|--------------|----|----|-----------|----|---------|--------------|
| **Project Initiation** | A | R | C | C | C | C | C | I | I | I | C |
| **Roadmap Planning** | C | A/R | C | C | C | C | C | I | I | I | C |
| **Sprint Planning** | C | C | R | R | A | C | C | I | C | I | I |
| **Feature Design** | C | C | R | C | C | A/R | C | I | I | I | C |
| **Development** | C | C | A/R | C | I | C | I | C | C | I | I |
| **Testing** | C | C | C | A/R | C | I | I | I | C | I | I |
| **Documentation** | C | C | C | C | I | C | C | A/R | I | C | I |
| **Deployment** | C | C | C | C | C | I | I | I | A/R | C | I |
| **Production Support** | C | I | C | I | I | I | I | C | C | A/R | I |
| **Retrospectives** | C | C | R | R | A | C | C | C | C | C | I |

**Legend:**
- **R (Responsible):** Performs the work
- **A (Accountable):** Ultimately answerable for completion
- **C (Consulted):** Provides input and expertise
- **I (Informed):** Kept up-to-date on progress
